GTA SAN

ANDREAS

Grand Theft Auto: San Andreas, commonly known as GTA San Andreas, is an action-adventure video game developed by Rockstar North and released in 2004. Set in the fictional state of San Andreas, the game follows the story of Carl "CJ" Johnson, who returns to his hometown of Los Santos after the murder of his mother. The game is renowned for its open-world gameplay, where players can freely roam the vast landscape, engage in various missions, and explore a detailed and immersive environment.

GTA 4

GTA IV has the best graphics for its time. It’s the only game where it’s actually fun to mess around and lose the cops. It has the best physics. It’s the only game that feels like it has a realistic atmosphere in the city. It’s the most difficult game to complete. It was incredible when it came out. It was a MASSIVE jump in graphics and gameplay from the previous main titles. The story was immersive. The map was limited, but still really fun. You feel like you are a person who lives in the city, where in GTA 5 and older games, it feels like you own the city. The story is by far the best in the series and personally is my favorite story in gaming period. Also, Niko is my favorite video game main character.

GTA V

Fun gameplay, fun story, fab characters, great radio stations, amazing view on mount chilad, golf! Oh and chop I love everything about 5, like I love everything in every other GTA game. They’re all great games with so much fun shit in every one of them. Not to mention the ability to swap between 3 main characters at any point you would like. For me it was how seamlessly it tied your open world activities to the story, like how Michael's dialogues during therapy sessions change depending on your preceding actions in the open world. This is also one of the many things that I think GTA V improved upon GTA IV. Balanced car physics, fun gameplay, fun missions, detailed city and ingame activities. it was perfect down to the last minute details

Battlefield 3

Here was one way Batt3 undeniably excelled over every other subsequent battlefield.In battlefield 3, you honestly feel like you're playing as some random dude in a world that feels like it actually exists. From campaign to multiplayer, there was a very distinct and consistent art direction present in the construction of the maps and characters. It was one of the first times a lot of us felt so completely immersed in a game with this level of gameplay freedom. The menus, the music, and I think even that blue filter helped contribute to that. A flash of seeing a jet scream over the top of the Alborz Mountains peak. nother flash of watching an American Assault stab a guy in a crater on Karkand and I was watching through my scoped M40A5, I could only see the Russian dude's arm as he was flipped over. I really miss those days of coming home from school login into my computer and then playing whatever the I want to play that day.
